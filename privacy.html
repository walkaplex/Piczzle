Privacy

Last updated: June 1, 2026

What Piczzle does

Piczzle turns photos into playable puzzles. You can use demo images, choose an image from your device, take a photo where supported, save a completed image, or create an unlisted puzzle link.

Photos

Photos you choose stay in the app unless you create a shared puzzle link or save/share an image through your device. Shared puzzle links upload a compressed puzzle image and puzzle size so another person can open and solve it.

Shared puzzle links

Shared links are unlisted. Anyone with the link can open the puzzle until it expires or is removed. Shared puzzles are designed to expire after 30 days.

Data stored for sharing

For shared puzzles, Piczzle stores a generated puzzle id, compressed image data, puzzle size, creation time, and expiration time. Piczzle does not require user accounts for the current private test flow.

Tester reports

If you report an issue, include only the information needed to reproduce it: device model, Android or browser version, screenshots if useful, what you tapped, and the shared puzzle link if the issue involved sharing.

Contact

For Piczzle support, tester feedback, or privacy questions, contact piczzle.support@gmail.com.
